Group Tabs — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 5, 2026

Group Tabs is a Chrome extension that organizes your open tabs into groups and lets you save and restore those groups. This policy describes what data it touches and where that data goes: nowhere.

What the extension accesses

  • Tab titles and URLs of your open tabs — needed to group tabs by domain or topic and to save group snapshots.
  • Tab group metadata (group names and colors) — needed to save and restore groups.

Where data is processed

Entirely on your device. Grouping uses either simple domain rules or Chrome's built-in on-device AI model (Gemini Nano). No tab data is ever sent to any server, API, or third party. The extension makes no network requests.

What is stored

Saved snapshots (group names, colors, and tab URLs) are stored in Chrome's local extension storage (chrome.storage.local) on your device only. They are not synced, transmitted, or shared.

What is collected

Nothing. No analytics, no telemetry, no cookies, no accounts, no personal data collection of any kind.

Data deletion

Delete individual snapshots from the extension popup at any time. Uninstalling the extension removes all stored data permanently.

Changes

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new "last updated" date.
