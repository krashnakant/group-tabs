Group Tabs privacy policy

Last updated: July 17, 2026

No Group Tabs server, analytics, or account. Tab organization runs on your device. Small manual sessions may sync through Chrome Sync; automatic recovery snapshots stay on this device.

What the extension accesses

  • Tab titles and URLs — to group tabs by site or topic and to save sessions.
  • Tab group metadata such as names and colors — to save and restore groups.

Where tab organization happens

Site grouping uses local rules. Topic grouping uses the browser's built-in on-device Prompt API when available. The extension does not send tab titles or URLs to a Group Tabs server, third-party API, or analytics service, and it contains no remote code.

What is stored

  • Manual saved sessions: group names, colors, and tab URLs are stored with chrome.storage.sync when the saved data fits Chrome's sync quota. Larger sessions stay in chrome.storage.local on this device.
  • Automatic recovery snapshots: the latest three snapshots stay in chrome.storage.local on this device and are not synced.
  • Settings: automation settings stay on this device. Preferred AI topic names may use Chrome Sync.

Chrome Sync is a browser service governed by your Chrome account and Google settings. Group Tabs does not operate that service.

What the developer collects

Nothing. Group Tabs has no analytics, telemetry, cookies, accounts, advertising, or developer-operated backend.

Data deletion

Delete any saved session from the popup; this removes it from whichever extension storage area contains it. Uninstalling removes device-local extension data. Chrome Sync data is also subject to your browser's sync and account controls.

Changes
